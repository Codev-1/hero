Awareness Campaigns — Renewable Energy Programs

Explore flagship schemes & eligibility — PM Surya Ghar, KUSUM, SMART, Green Open Access, Virtual & Group Net Metering.

1
PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana
Rooftop Solar for households

Basic policy introduction

Provision of rooftop solar systems to households to provide free electricity and reduce dependency on the grid.

Benefits

Free monthly electricity units, lower bills, long-term savings and clean energy adoption.

Eligibility

Residential households with suitable rooftops and valid electricity connections.

2
KUSUM Scheme
Solar support for agriculture

Basic policy introduction

Promotes solar pumps and grid-connected renewable systems to empower farmers and reduce diesel use.

Benefits

Subsidized solar pumps, irrigation savings, and potential income from surplus energy.

Eligibility

Registered farmers, cooperatives, panchayats with agricultural land.

3
SMART Program
Smart renewable integration

Basic policy introduction

Supports modern renewable integrations—smart metering, energy management, and urban energy projects.

Benefits

Higher efficiency, better monitoring, and incentives for smart deployments.

Eligibility

Urban local bodies, smart city projects, implemented plans.

4
Green Open Access
Direct purchase of renewable power

Basic policy introduction

Allows consumers—especially commercial/industrial—to buy renewable energy directly from generators.

Benefits

Lower costs, supplier freedom, and greenhouse gas reductions.

Eligibility

Entities meeting minimum load criteria (often ≥100 kW) and fulfilling regulatory requirements.

5
Virtual Net Metering
Off-site solar credits

Basic policy introduction

Consumers subscribe to off-site solar plants and receive virtual credit allocation against their bills.

Benefits

No rooftop required, scalable, and allows access to solar benefits across locations.

Eligibility

Group housing societies, institutions, and commercial consumers without rooftop space.

6
Group Net Metering
Shared generation for multiple meters

Basic policy introduction

Enables multiple consumers within the same premises to share energy from a common solar installation.

Benefits

Shared cost model, efficient distribution, and reduced collective bills.

Eligibility

RWAs, apartment complexes, institutions with multiple meters.
